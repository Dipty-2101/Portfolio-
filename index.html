The Earth Remembers: Hermès Presents Mitti aur Reshma

A fictional collection imagined as a quiet dialogue between the ateliers of Paris and the block-print masters of Jaipur — written to demonstrate an understanding of luxury brand communication.

Paris, France  ·  March 2026  ·  For Immediate Release

Some stories are not invented. They are uncovered. Found in the crease of a hand-blocked print, in the silence between two cultures meeting for the first time. Mitti aur Reshma, the latest expression from Maison Hermès, is one such story. Born from a quiet dialogue between the ateliers of Paris and the block-print masters of Jaipur, this collection does not seek to impress. It seeks to endure.

Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication. And in a world that rewards noise, Hermès has always chosen the harder path, to say less, and mean more.

The Collection

Mitti aur Reshma. Soil and Silk. The name carries the weight of everything this collection is. Earth pigments drawn from the desert landscapes of Rajasthan meet the house's signature silk in scarves, leather goods, and ready-to-wear pieces that feel less like fashion and more like memory. Each piece emerged from a months-long exchange between Hermès petits mains in Paris and artisan families in Jaipur who have practiced bagh and dabu block-printing across generations. No pattern was transferred. No motif was borrowed. Everything was co-created, slowly, with intention.

The Craft Dialogue

Hermès has always known that true luxury is not manufactured. It is inherited, refined, and passed forward with care. In Mitti aur Reshma, that inheritance is doubled. Rajasthani block-printing, one of India's oldest and most disciplined textile traditions, operates by the same principles that have guided this house since 1837, patience, the primacy of the human hand, and a deep refusal to hurry beauty. What emerged from this exchange is a visual language that belongs entirely to this moment, and to no single culture.

Creative Voice

"We did not go to Jaipur to be inspired. We went to be taught. What came back with us was not a collection. It was a conversation, one we hope continues long after the last piece finds its home." Lea Moreau, Hermes Artistic Director

Notes to Editors

Mitti aur Reshma will be unveiled at a private presentation in Paris on 15 April 2026, ahead of a global rollout across select Hermès boutiques. A limited atelier edition, hand-signed by the participating Jaipur artisan families, will be available exclusively in-store. High-resolution imagery and press materials are available upon request.
